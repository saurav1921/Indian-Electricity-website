Our tips to save electricity

Switch to LED Bulbs
They're far more efficient than incandescent lightbulbs, last longer and can provide a cleaner, more natural looking light.
Install smart switches
There's loads available, from simple timed switches to ones that can be controlled remotely from your smartphone.
Turn it off at the switch
Leaving electronics on standby means that electricity is still being used so they can be turned on at a moment’s notice.
Turning these appliances off at the wall stops this from happening–you could even use a smart switch as mentioned in the point above.
Clean the coils on your fridge
Chances are you’ve never bothered to do this. The coils on the back of your fridge can get really dirty which reduces efficiency, so aim to give them a clean at least once a year.
Keep your fridge and freezer full
A full fridge-freezer needs less energy to keep cold as the items inside maintain the temperature.
